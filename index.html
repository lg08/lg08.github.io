About me

I'm a senior at Princeton University, earning a Bachelors of Science and Engineering in computer science, with a certificate (minor) in Statistics and Machine Learning.

Outside of the classroom I keep myself busy as captain of Princeton's Division I soccer team and president of the Princeton Charter Club.

My interests in the field of computer science are quite varied, with my favorite projects ranging from creating a shell from scratch, to training machine learning models to estimate the energy output from solar panels in a certain area based off of satellite images. I have an incredible passion for using technology to make the world a cleaner, more sustainable place, and love to philosophize about the interactions between humans and technology.

Born in Sacramento, CA as the youngest of 2 siblings, I'm a proud Asian-American with family roots coming from a small village outside of Canton.

  • library_books

    Kindle to Notion Syncer

    Overview:

    An open-source solution to automatically sync all kindle highlights and annotations into a Notion database. A free alternative to a paid solution known as Readwise. Currently being used by about 50 people. Video overview and tutorial here.


    What I learned/loved:

    I absolutely loved some of the feedback I got from people who had been looking for a free solution like this before and hadn't had any luck. I'm a huge open-source enthusiast and it made me happy to contribute to the community even in a very small way and give people who can't afford Readwise a free alternative. I also learned a lot about working with different API's and how to navigate through unclear documentation.

  • solar_power

    A Step Towards a Better Solar Panel Development Tracking System

    Overview:

    This paper describes the process of training and using a machine learning model to estimate the energy output in a certain area based solely off satellite images in the area. I trained this model and wrote this paper on it as part of my junior independent work at Princeton University.


    What I learned/loved:

    I loved how this project combined my passion for renewable energy with my passion for computer science and machine learning. This was also my first substantial project using Machine Learning models outside of a theoretical environment so I learned a lot about dealing with real world data and real world models.
